East Lampung Regency's Jabung sub-region and its coastal strip sit at the
intersection of two development models the regency wanted to pursue at
once: agropolitan zoning inland, built around agricultural production
centers, and minapolitan zoning along the coast, built around fisheries.
Deciding which specific areas should anchor each model — rather than
applying both labels uniformly across the whole coastline — needed a way to
compare sub-districts against each other on more than just which side of an
administrative line they sit on.

## Macro–micro gravity analysis

The analytical core was a gravity analysis run at two scales. At the macro
level, the analysis modeled the pull each population and economic center in
East Lampung exerts on the surrounding region — the same logic gravity
models use in retail and transportation planning, applied here to weigh
which existing centers were strong enough to anchor a development zone
around them. At the micro level, the same logic was applied within the
Jabung and coastal study area itself, comparing individual sub-districts
against each other on population, economic activity, and accessibility to
identify which ones had the underlying pull to function as a secondary
center rather than remain peripheral to a larger one. IBM SPSS handled the
statistical weighting and scoring behind both scales; ArcGIS carried the
spatial side — building the base layers, running the zonal comparisons, and
producing the final thematic maps.

## Weighing policy against potential

A gravity score alone doesn't decide zoning — it has to sit against
existing policy direction and demographic reality. The analysis compared
each sub-district's gravity ranking with East Lampung's existing spatial
policy commitments and its demographic profile, so a sub-district with a
strong gravity score but a demographic base misaligned with agropolitan or
minapolitan activity wouldn't get flagged as a priority center just because
the numbers looked good in isolation. That cross-check was what turned a
purely quantitative ranking into a set of thematic recommendations the
regency's spatial plan could actually adopt.

## Output

The deliverable fed directly into East Lampung's Jabung and Coastal Area
Development Plan, produced for Bappeda Lampung Timur (the regency's regional
development planning agency) alongside a related regional drinking-water
strategy document for the same area — giving the coastal strip a thematic
base grounded in where development pressure and potential actually sit,
rather than a zoning map drawn from administrative boundaries alone.
